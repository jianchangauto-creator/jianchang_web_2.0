FAQ

Common questions about quoting, lead times, prototyping, and how we work.

What do I need to provide for a quote?

A drawing (PDF / STEP), material, quantity, and tolerance requirements are ideal. No drawing yet? Just tell us OD, length, material, and application — we can assess feasibility first.

How long does prototyping take?

Typical prototype lead time: 5–10 business days after order confirmation. Complex parts or special materials may take longer — we'll specify the lead time in our quote.

Is there a minimum order quantity?

No minimum — we accept both prototype and production orders. Many customers start with a small sample run to confirm quality and specs before scaling up.

What's the smallest diameter you can machine?

Minimum OD Ø0.7mm, lengths from 0.3–200mm. Parts outside this range can still be submitted for evaluation — some cases are possible depending on the drawing.

Can you provide quality reports?

Yes — we can provide first-article inspection reports, lot sampling records, and environmental compliance documentation (RoHS, SGS) per customer requirements.

Do you accept international inquiries?

Yes. Please send drawings by email — we respond in English or Chinese. International orders can ship by air or sea freight.
