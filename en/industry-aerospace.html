Why Aerospace Supply Chains
Choose Jian Chang

The exact challenges of superalloy machining are where Jian Chang's Swiss-type turning and years of experience excel.

  • Inconel 718, A286, and Titanium are difficult to machine with fast tool wear — most general turning shops won't take these jobs. Jian Chang has proven, stable production experience.
  • Aerospace orders are often low-volume, high-mix, with many part numbers running in parallel — exactly the service model Jian Chang specializes in, with no minimum order quantity.
  • Machining range of Ø0.7–20mm covers the precision studs, shafts, fasteners, and small structural parts common in aerospace supply chains.
  • Heat treatment, surface treatment (e.g. passivation), and sampling or full inspection can be coordinated per customer drawing and specification.
  • One supplier from prototype through production — simplifying part-number and supplier management complexity in aerospace supply chains.

Recommended Materials for Aerospace Parts

Comparison of material properties and suitable applications for aerospace parts.

Material Properties Suitable Parts Notes
Inconel 718 Heat resistant, corrosion resistant, high strength High-temp studs, turbine-related small parts Difficult to machine, fast tool wear
A286 High-temp oxidation resistant, high-strength fastener steel High-temp fasteners Commonly used in engine-adjacent parts
Titanium Ti-6Al-4V Lightweight, high strength, biocompatible Structural parts, shafts Low thermal conductivity — requires controlled machining parameters
17-4PH (SUS630) High strength, corrosion resistant, heat-treatable Fasteners, small structural parts Combines strength and corrosion resistance
7075-T6 High-strength aluminum alloy, lightweight Structural shafts, housings Strength close to steel at a fraction of the weight
SCM440 High-strength alloy steel, fatigue resistant UAV shafts, drive components Can be heat-treated for higher hardness
